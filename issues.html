Issues that matter to me

Quality municipal services: water, sewer, garbage, police, and fire

There are three bond propositions included on the November 8th general election ballot, which address all of these issues.

I have toured all the facilities and agree that improvements are necessary. Public tours will be offered and I encourage you all to take advantage of those opportunities.

Welfare of Citizens

I am an outdoors person and I walk wherever I can, but it is dangerous.

  • • We need sidewalks, bike lanes, and traffic laws to protect cyclists and pedestrians.

  • • Parks need to be improved, and expanded where possible.

  • • Neighborhoods must be protected from random zoning changes. Families, and the homes they live in, are the lifeblood of this town.

Economic Development

We have a great town with wonderful attributes: beautiful scenery, clean water, and an educated, productive workforce. Businesses want to be here because we have a lot to offer. Therefore, I do not support the creation of an Economic Development Corporation funded with tax dollars. I believe we can spur economic development by keeping taxes low, housing affordable, and focusing on what really matters - our community.

Change is inevitable, and we must revise the Development Code to provide better tools to manage the growth that is coming so we can help determine what this town will look like for future generations. Haphazard policies and spur of the moment decisions based on selfish, short term goals do not provide a solid foundation for a city. It is our duty to make responsible decisions that protect and promote the quaity of life for all citizens.
